3 · The privacy dial — one clearing, three viewers

The single sharpest thing about DrEX: the same clearing, over the same verified kernel, shown to three different viewers. Open shows everyone everything. Shielded makes the world see only the proof + the public volume — the per-order flows live in the STARK witness (the blur below is exactly what the proof hides). Dark keeps the inputs encrypted — no solver, committee, or enclave ever sees an order. Fair, conserving, no-mint, proven is identical at every tier; only what the world sees moves.

Honest scope. Open is ≈ the DrEX that runs today. Shielded is the Stage-1 engine being built — its clearing-level reveal-nothing theorem is named research (the hiding-FRI statistical-ZK floor). Dark is the FHE frontier, bounded by the real FHE performance envelope (uniform-price / Cert-F products, bounded N, minute cadence). Each tier's label is exactly what it is.
